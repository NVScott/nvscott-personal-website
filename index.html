Hey, I'm Nessa. I'm what happens when you can't decide between a going into a field that's technical, people-oriented, or creative -- you pick one that's all three. Human-Computer Interaction is understanding the cognitive science behind the design. It's knowing who your users are, finding out what they want and need, and finding ways to fulfill those needs that are efficient, intuitive, and elegant. It's a lot more than a good color scheme and pretty typography, crucial as those are. It's predicting mistakes, and designing your app to flow in a way that makes them rare.

It's not just research and principles, though. Beyond personas, wifeframes, and heuristic evaluations I've got technical skills, too. I'm comfortable using python, html5, css, javascript (plus common tools like jquery, bootstrap, angular, and ionic). I'm also familiar with data-visualization tools and techniques, non-relational databases, and working with huge datasets to tell a story. I have had training in project management, and done groupwork with groups of many sizes, as both leader and subordinate. I also am currently doing research on the ethics of privacy and data-collection. I am mostly interested in UI design, but enjoy discovering, researching, and designing all elements of the user experience.

Check out my Projects page to see my research, mockups, and designs. Feel free to review my resume, and drop me an email or call. Thanks!
